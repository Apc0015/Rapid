# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RAPID launches for startup founders and lean teams. They need to find operating context, make decisions, complete work, and configure company services without learning separate tools.

## Product Purpose

RAPID is a governed startup operating workspace. It brings product delivery, customer work, meetings, projects, records, reporting, and AI-assisted intelligence into one tenant-aware workspace. Success means a founder can move from signal to accountable action with confidence.

Customers begin with a startup operating model rather than a generic empty portal. It provides the initial work areas and services needed for product, growth, customer, finance, and delivery work. Administrators can expand the configuration as the company grows.

For private, on-prem, and hybrid deployments, the tenant-configured AI runtime is a product control: RAPID blocks cloud model providers where the policy prohibits cloud egress and defaults to a local Ollama runtime. The governed organization-data path uses that tenant runtime. Older direct cloud connector routes are disabled by default while tenant-scoped connector migration and full at-rest encryption remain deployment hardening work, not claims of completed compliance.

RAPID Chat is the continuous intelligence surface inside that workspace. A page-level question opens the same governed conversation with the current operational context; users can investigate startup or work-area data, review evidence, and continue with follow-up questions without losing history.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and trustworthy. The interface should feel as considered as high-quality Apple software while remaining practical for dense operational work.

## Anti-references

Avoid dark developer-console styling, decorative gradients, noisy card grids, oversized marketing language, low-contrast text, and unfamiliar controls.

## Design Principles

- Put the current decision or task ahead of product decoration.
- Make dense organizational data feel ordered, not crowded.
- Use a small, predictable component vocabulary across every portal surface.
- Make governance and AI assistance visible without making the interface feel technical.
- Preserve user flow through responsive, accessible defaults.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA contrast and interaction standards, support keyboard navigation and visible focus, respect reduced-motion preferences, and maintain responsive behavior from mobile through desktop.
