# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Enterprise employees at all levels of an organization — from individual contributors (employees, analysts) through managers, department heads, division heads, C-suite, and platform admins. Primary context: desktop, workplace, daytime. Users are professionals who trust the platform with sensitive organizational data. The primary task on any given screen is querying, reviewing, or acting on AI-generated intelligence scoped to their role.

## Product Purpose

RAPID is a privacy-first enterprise AI platform that gives every department its own intelligent agent — trained on the organization's own documents and databases, governed by role-based column-level rules defined in a constitution file. Raw data never reaches the LLM. The platform enforces human-in-the-loop approval for consequential AI actions. Success looks like a department head who can query "What's our Q3 retention by segment?" and receive a governed, auditable answer in seconds — without IT involvement.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Controlled. Trusted. The tone is expert-confident without being cold. RAPID is the kind of tool that makes a CFO feel like they have a senior analyst available at all times — not a chatbot. Voice is direct, specific, and action-oriented. No filler. No exclamation points.

## Anti-references

- **ChatGPT / Perplexity**: Avoid the minimal AI-chat-centric layout where chat is the only surface. RAPID has departments, projects, actions, governance, people, and an approval pipeline — it is a full operating system, not a chat interface.
- No consumer-social warmth (Notion, Slack style).
- No legacy enterprise bloat (Salesforce, AWS Console aesthetic).

## Design Principles

1. **Governance is the product.** Every design decision should reinforce that RAPID takes data access seriously. Role clarity, audit trails, and approval states are first-class UI citizens — not footnotes.
2. **Precision over decoration.** Every element earns its place. No gradients for aesthetics, no icons for color, no animations for delight alone. Motion and color serve information.
3. **Command center, not dashboard.** Users should feel in control, not observed. The interface should feel like a cockpit — density-appropriate, scannable, actionable.
4. **Role as context.** The interface should adapt meaningfully to role. An employee's view and a CEO's view are different products with the same shell.
5. **Linear-inspired execution.** Clean geometry, tight spacing, confident dark theme. Tool-forward, not marketing-forward.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a baseline. Enterprise context means keyboard navigation is non-negotiable. Reduced motion preference must be respected. Color is never the sole signal for status (always paired with label or icon).
